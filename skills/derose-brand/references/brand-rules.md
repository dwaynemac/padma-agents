# DeROSE Brand Rules

## Palette

- Primary: `#040e85`
  Use for structural surfaces, navigation, active states, and signature brand moments.
- Secondary: `#ffffff`
  Use as the default canvas and the main source of visual calm.
- Tertiary: `#c69c6c`
  Use as a restrained accent for highlights, editorial support, or premium cues.

Do not let gold take over the page. Blue should carry structure; gold should punctuate.

## Typography

- Headings should feel editorial, poised, and slightly cultural rather than purely technical.
- Use `Sanomat Web` for `h1`, `h2`, and brand-title treatments when the font is available.
- The observed source defines `Sanomat Web` via:
  - `Sanomat-LightItalic-Web.woff2`
  - `Sanomat-LightItalic-Web.woff`
  - `font-weight: 300`
  - `font-style: italic`
- Use `Helvetica, sans-serif` for body text, controls, metadata, and dense UI.
- Preserve strong contrast between expressive headings and practical interface copy.
- If the exact font files are unavailable in a target project, preserve the same typographic role: light italic editorial headings and Helvetica-like practical body text.

## Layout and Density

- Favor light canvases with clear sectioning and visible navigation.
- Keep layouts orderly and measured. Avoid both cramped dashboards and oversized airy startup heroes.
- Use horizontal grouping when showing curated content or related modules.
- Keep containers disciplined and aligned; avoid scattered floating elements.

## CTAs

- Keep CTAs modest in scale.
- Prefer repeated contextual prompts over one oversized conversion button.
- Buttons should look intentional and useful, not salesy.
- In the observed Cervino implementation, CTA buttons use the brown accent as fill with white text, then invert toward a white background on hover.

## Navigation

- Navigation should remain visible and orienting.
- Use strong top-level navigation when the product has multiple major areas.
- Mobile navigation can rely on a bottom bar or off-canvas patterns if access remains immediate.

## Cards and Components

- Prefer soft corners, light shadows, and simple hierarchy.
- Keep component styling consistent across a page.
- Let content, spacing, and typography do most of the work.
- Avoid ornamental chrome, layered badges, and excessive icon treatment.

## Motion

- Use subtle fades, small hover shifts, and quiet transitions.
- Motion should support clarity, not compete for attention.

## Do

- Make the interface feel elegant, calm, and competent.
- Use blue as the anchoring color and gold as a supporting accent.
- Keep headings expressive and body copy practical.
- Maintain a premium tone through restraint and consistency.

## Don't

- Do not drift into generic SaaS styling.
- Do not overuse gradients, glassmorphism, or neon accents.
- Do not make headings feel utilitarian or developer-default.
- Do not swap out Sanomat-style heading treatment for generic product typography.
- Do not turn every surface into a card grid if a cleaner sectional layout works better.
